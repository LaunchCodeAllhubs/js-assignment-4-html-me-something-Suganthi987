    Niagara Falls in NewYork and Ontario

    I had the chance to visit Niagra Falls, both on the American side and on the Canadian side as well. On the American side, I rode the Maid of the Mist and the views were breath-taking!
    A couple of years later, I went in the Canadian side, enjoying Behind The Falls and walking along the Whitewater Boardwalk.The falls lighted up at night and there were fireworks which was unforgettable!

    Montreal and Old Quebec City, Canada

    I visited the bio-dome of Montreal! which had over 800 plants and over 2500 animals in their natural habitat. It was a place of wonder where I got to experience and learn the natural habitats of various plants and animals.
    I then traveled to Old Quebec City immersing myself in the beauty of old stone buildings and cobbled streets.

    Toronto in Canada

    I had the opportunity to enjoy gorgeous downtown Toronto and also visited CN towers which had quite the view from up-top!

    Smoky Mountains in Gatlingburg, Tennesse, USA

    Gatlingburg had amazing views of lush smoky mountain ranges. I went on a few trails and drive throughs to get beautiful views of the mountain ranges.
    I also went to Gatlingburg city and Pigeonforge wherein there were lots of acitivies to do in the evening!

    Amusement Parks at Orlando, Florida, USA

    I had the opportunity to visit DisneyWorld, Magic Kingdom, Universal Studios, Sea World, Animal Kingdom and Epcot, each of which were a world onto themselves.
    I had a blast visiting each one of them. The rides were amazing and it is a must see to anyone who visits USA!

    Golden Gate Bridge in SanFrancisco, Calfornia, USA

    Golden bridge is one of the iconic bridges in California! I got to watch the bridge with mists and fog covering it which gave it a unique and mystic look!

    Grand Canyon in Arizona, USA

    The Grand Canyon offers one of the most visible examples of a worldwide geological phenomenon known as the Great Unconformity, in which 250 million-year-old rock strata lie back-to-back with 1.2 billion-year-old rocks, which is a delight to see aswell!
